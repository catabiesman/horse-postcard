## Find a key to our categories below

Source identifier: Refers to the item’s unique identifier in our cataloguing system. 

- Example: p-2-01

Title: Captures a brief description of the postcard design, the location depicted if applicable, and the postmark year in parentheses. 

- Example title: Postcard of state capitol building in Carson City, Nevada (1942)

Creator: Reflects the name (as written on the card) of the person or persons who sent the postcard. 

- Example creator: Oliver Solseth. 

Description: Captures the style of card (illustration or photograph); specifies if it is in color or black and white; and offers a brief description of the scene depicted on the card. 

- Example: Illustration. Color. Capitol building with trees and walkway

Subjects: Reflects locations related to the card of both postmark and recipient. Reflects the style of image depicted varying from building, nature-scape, city-scape, to portrait. 

- Example: Utah, Ogden, United States, Maine, Scarboro, Building

Location: The city, region, and country of the card’s destination address. 

- Example: United States, Maine, Scarboro

This corresponds to the categories of longitude and latitude which are reflected in the map.

Language: Reflects the language of the postcard sender’s writing. 

- Example: English  

Source: Reflects the body which hosts these postcards. 

- Example: Juan Cobo Postcard Collection
